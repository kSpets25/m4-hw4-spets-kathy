About Milton Glaser

Milton Glaser was born in (1929)in the Bronx of new York. He was born to immigrants who migrated here from Hungry. He attended The Cooper Union for the Advancement of Schience and Art studio on a full scholarship. He also studied in Bologna, Italy. Glaser co-founded Push Pin studios, a New York Company created by a group of graphic designers that created and expnded a type of collaborative graphic expression of visual art and unique styles.

He started his own company called Milton Glaser, Inc. in 1974. Glaser was also part of the WBMG which is a design publication firm. Throughout his evoling career he contributed some of the most iconic icons, posters, artwork,printwork and even architectural design,that makes him one of the greatest, most well know, graphic designmers in United States history.

Iconic Works of Art

Artwork created by Milton Glaser has earned him a distincitve place in graphic design history. He has been able to have his own featured art shows, including Paris and New York. His work is displayed in museums and he has received many awards, including the most honorable Lifetime Achievement Award in 2004. Milton Glaser contributed not only to creating iconic graphic designs but also shared his knowledge through essays and interviews. His contributions to educating others in the area of art and design is quite profound.

According to cooperhewitt.org:

"Glaser is best known among designers for his illustrated posters and album covers. His Dylan poster (1966) became an enduring symbol of a unique era in American pop culture, while his posters for Olivetti mashed together his triple love for Renaissance art, Italian design, and American comic books."
